# UNGA-Climate-Discourse-Analysis
**The Politicization of "Climate" in UNGA Discourse (1980–2020)" instead of "The Evolving Politicization of "Climate" in the United Nations General Assembly Discourse: A Corpus-Based Analysis (1980-2020)
**
This repository contains my term paper for the course Doing Corpus Linguistics in the M.A. Data and Discourse Studies program at Technische Universität Darmstadt.

This research project employs Corpus Linguistics to investigate how the term “climate” evolved from a largely neutral concept into a politically charged term in United Nations General Assembly (UNGA) debates between 1980 and 2020.

Using corpus linguistic methods in CQPweb, the analysis focuses on:
- Frequency and relative frequency trends over time
- Geopolitical distribution across UN country groups
- Collocation analysis to trace semantic and political shifts

The results show a clear transformation of “climate” from general diplomatic language in the 1980s to institutionalized, action-oriented discourse in the 2010s (e.g., mitigation, adaptation, resilience, UNFCCC, Paris).

Methods:
- Lemma-based frequency analysis
- Temporal and geopolitical distribution analysis
- Log-ratio based collocation analysis (±5 word window)
- Quantitative corpus statistics combined with qualitative interpretation
The study is based on the UN General Assembly Debates corpus (1970–2020).
